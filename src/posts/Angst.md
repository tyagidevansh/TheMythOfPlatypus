Ever stare at a blank wall, heart pounding, thoughts racing over an uncertain sense of reality? This feeling of unknown strips away the freedom of choice.

Angst, or anxiety as defined by Danish philosopher Søren Kierkegaard, is the dizziness of freedom.

Is there a way to always choose the correct decision? We are exposed to nearly infinite unknown possibilities that become known in real time through choices and actions.

We are driven by anxiety to make choices that eliminate it, but alas, we are savages chasing our tails.

Part of the reason I am fascinated by Kierkegaard's tale of angst is how he perceives it. He beleives Angst to not be

leap of faith (Spider verse reference)

Passion
